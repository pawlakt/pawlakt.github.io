I love playing cards.
When I first saw LLMs in action (yeah, ChatGPT back in 2022), I was amazed at how smoothly it could answer my silly questions.
I remember being pretty skeptical — I like to call myself a “realist,” but my friends would say I lean more toward skepticism — and all the AI hype at the time didn’t really convince me.
But then, recently, something changed. I can’t point to the exact moment (maybe one of [Andrej Karpathy’s](https://www.youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy) YouTube videos?), but I realized the deck had been reshuffled — and things had changed for good.


I think I played my hand pretty well in the previous round — but that round is over. Feels like I need to reinvent myself. Again.
When I moved from mechanical engineering into the IT world, I thought that transition would be permanent but turns out — it wasn’t!
I still remember the excitement when I first dived into [C++](https://books.google.nl/books/about/C++_Primer_Plus.html?id=zuyAIZ9ZIskC&redir_esc=y) by Stephen Prata back in 2017 (time flies, seriously!!!).
My brain kind of exploded — I had so much fun writing trivial console apps in C++, and before that I didn’t even realize my curiosity could stretch that far. That curiosity carried me through my career — across different roles, different projects, all that. But somewhere along the way, that initial excitement started to fade a bit (common thing I believe).

That’s why I’m so excited now — we’re all stepping into a new era. Not just in IT, but probably as a civilization.
The closest comparison for me is the Industrial Revolution — something that completely reshapes how we think about work and life.
It took me a while to really get it, but this shift we’re part of feels inevitable — and unstoppable. So I’m betting my most valuable asset on it: time.
I’m all in now — aiming to become an “AI expert” (sounds sooo LinkedIn-ish, kinda cringe sorry!).

I see a lot of people are extremely concerned about this transition.
Sometimes I am trying to imagine how people felt when they first realized they didn’t have to sew clothes by hand anymore — that machines could do it for them, and in result - some of them may felt kind of useless / redundant.
My opinion - people are scared mostly because they don’t understand what’s going on (as usual).

Instead of seeing this as a threat, we should look at it as endless opportunity across pretty much every area of life and the economy.

Sure, there are risks that come with this technology — I’m aware of that. But again, it’s not the first time humanity has had to deal with something powerful and a bit scary.

I mean… imagine seeing fire for the first time 😄...

And at the end — imagine how far we still have to go to reach some “higher level of intelligence”… if matrix and vector multiplications was enough to turn our world upside down 😄
